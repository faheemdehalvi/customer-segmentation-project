# Customer Segmentation Report

## Executive Summary
This report summarizes the findings from the customer segmentation project aimed at identifying distinct customer segments based on purchasing behavior. The analysis leverages clustering techniques to group customers and generate actionable insights for targeted marketing strategies.

## Data Overview
The dataset used for this analysis is sourced from the UCI Online Retail Dataset, containing transactional data of customers. Key features include customer ID, invoice number, product details, quantity purchased, and total price.

## Methodology
The project followed a structured approach:

1. **Data Exploration**: Initial exploration of the dataset to understand its structure, identify missing values, and visualize key trends.
2. **Data Cleaning**: Addressed missing values, removed cancelled orders, and prepared the dataset for further analysis.
3. **Feature Engineering**: Created a Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (RFM) table to quantify customer behavior and scaled features for clustering.
4. **Modeling**: Applied K-means and DBSCAN clustering algorithms to identify customer segments, followed by PCA for dimensionality reduction and visualization.
5. **Insights Generation**: Analyzed the clusters to profile customer segments and provide recommendations for marketing strategies.

## Key Findings
- **Customer Segments**: The analysis identified several distinct customer segments, each with unique purchasing behaviors.
- **Segment Profiles**: Detailed profiles for each segment were created, highlighting characteristics such as average purchase frequency, recency, and monetary value.
- **Marketing Recommendations**: Based on the segment profiles, tailored marketing strategies were proposed to enhance customer engagement and retention.

## Visualizations
Figures illustrating the clustering results and segment profiles are included in the accompanying figures directory. These visualizations provide a clear representation of the customer segments and their characteristics.

## Conclusion
The customer segmentation analysis provides valuable insights that can inform targeted marketing efforts. By understanding the distinct behaviors of different customer segments, the business can optimize its marketing strategies to improve customer satisfaction and drive sales growth.

## Future Work
Further analysis could explore the impact of external factors on customer behavior and refine segmentation strategies based on additional data sources. Continuous monitoring of customer segments will also be essential to adapt to changing market dynamics.
